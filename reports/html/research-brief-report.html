Should a small team start with a local-first AI research assistant before building a cloud collaboration product?

Start with a local-first research assistant. Delay cloud collaboration until the local workflow proves repeated use and quality.

Recommendations

  • Start with a local-first research assistant. Delay cloud collaboration until the local workflow proves repeated use and quality.

Evidence / Claims

SRC-001

Customer Interview Notes

Early users prefer tools that run on their own machine when testing with notes, drafts, or customer documents.
SRC-002

Prototype Usage Log Summary

Users completed local import and summarization more often than team-sharing setup. Most drop-offs happened when configuration required accounts or remote services.
SRC-003

Founder Strategy Note

The initial product should prove quality and repeat use before introducing mobile, team, or marketplace complexity.
CLM-001

A local-first research assistant is the lower-friction first product direction for this team.

Interview notes show privacy and local-first preferences. Prototype logs show higher completion for local import and summarization. Strategy note emphasizes quality before expansion.

Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002, SRC-003

CLM-002

Cloud collaboration should be deferred until repeated local use is proven.

Drop-offs increased when remote setup or account configuration was required. Founder strategy note warns against overbuilding team and marketplace complexity.

Sources: SRC-002, SRC-003

CLM-003

The current evidence is not enough to decide enterprise readiness.

The usage sample is small and may not represent enterprise buyers.

Sources: SRC-002
